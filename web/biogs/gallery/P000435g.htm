Singing Bridges: The Millennium Bridge
Title:Singing Bridges: The Millennium Bridge
Type:MP3 File
Description:

The Millennium Bridge was recorded in 2002 after the bridge reopened once the ‘synchronous lateral excitation’ had been fixed. The recording is made on minidisk, using Schaller Oyster Piezo Microphones and edited using pro-tools. The cables are thin wires along the walkway with a high-pitched chattering sound.

Digital Versions:MP3 File

Singing Bridges: Glebe Island Bridge
Title:Singing Bridges: Glebe Island Bridge
Type:MP3 File
Description:

This recording of the then Glebe Island Bridge (now called Anzac Bridge) was made in 1995 before construction of the bridge was completed. The material was recorded on DAT and edited on analogue 4-track with no added effects or processing of the sound. The deep resonance of the sounds is partly due to the length and tension of the cables and partly the recording technique, thanks to the technical assistance of sound engineers from ABC Radio’s “The Listening Room.” Placing C-Ducer contact microphones inside the multi-strand cables before encasing them in the final polyethylene covering which damps the vibrations.
