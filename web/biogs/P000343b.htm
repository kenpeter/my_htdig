Calvinesque Connections (1999)
Related EntriesAudio Visual Gallery
Performance
Location: The Tower, Ripponlea Estate, Victoria, Australia
By Warren Burt
Details
A performance for unamplified voice and live computer by Warren Burt in The Tower, Ripponlea Estate, run daily from 21 - 28 November as part of “Recent Ruins”. The following is the text handed out the audience at each performance:

Italo Calvino's Invisible Cities concerns a series of imaginary conversations between the Venetian merchant Marco Polo and the Chinese emperor, Kublai Khan. Up in this tower, I'm playing music on a computer which is tuned in the ancient Greek modes of the philosopher, mathematician, and music theorist Archytas of Tarentum (in southern Italy). Archytas was a friend of Plato's, and he proposed a very unique and beautiful version of the ancient Greek modal system. It may have been this system, in fact, that inspired his friend Plato to write, perhaps satirically (?), that "any innovation in music is a danger to the whole state and ought to be prohibited." The melodies of this music are produced by the use of various chaos mathematical systems. And for each day of my performance, the music will be in a different one of Archytas' modes, using three of his scales in that mode. The music that I'm playing uses the sounds of a ukelele processed so that they sound, to my ear, anyway, like a Chinese or Japanese string instrument. An imaginary orientalism made with ancient Mediterranean materials! And this faux ancient/oriental music is then augmented with my reading of ancient Chinese and Japanese poetry. If Archytas is my Marco Polo, then Li Po, Han Shan, Po Chu-I and Basho are my Kublai Khan. And in this English style fantasy tower, in Australia, ancient Mediterranean and Chinese materials are combined, live and improvisationally. What could be more in the spirit of the cultural fantasies of Italo Calvino?
