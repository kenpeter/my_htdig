Alsop, Roger
Related EntriesPublished Resources
Artist, Academic, Composer and Sound Designer
URL: The home page for this entity is located at http://people.smartchat.net.au/~rogeralsop/
Musical composition is a small subset of sound composition, which makes up a very small part of the audio world we live in. As an artist working with sound my main goal is to draw conscious relationships between the audio world of the listener and they themselves.

Therefore designs I make at present are attempts to engage the listener more deeply in their world through the emotional states our ubiquitous sonic environment creates.

I also work in the performance arts as a collaborator with other artists. In this work my goal is to create acoustic worlds in which there is a fully integrated artwork comprised of the parts.

Technological Approach

Technology is the least of my concerns while conceptualising, developing, and then presenting the work. The hardware for presentation i s whatever I can afford, be that through a funding body or self-funded. Recording equipment is what eve is handy or I can borrow if I need a specific thing. The software is usually Max/MSP, Pro Tools, Cool Edit, AudioMulch or whatever takes my fancy for the project.An example of a piece is “Someone” performed as an installation at the En-Red-O festival in Barcelona 1999. Here 4 stereo systems played 4 CDs with up to 5 tracks that were selected at random. The eight speakers were placed equidistant from each other on a horizontal plane and the audience moved around in the sonic space creating their own personal mix of the 8 possible audio signals. This piece has been presented in a number of forms, each fitting to the budget and the social/physical environment.
