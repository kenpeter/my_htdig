Cooper, Rod
 
Artist, Sound Designer, Performer, Improviser, Curator and Instrument Maker
My interest in sound and music became more acute while completing my sculpture degree in 1988. The sound of metal workshops developed my awareness of the possibilities for visual sculpture to render space with sound. I began construction of several musical instruments and recorded improvisations in the workshop.

During 1989-1998 I ran a small metal work business, creating furniture, architectural fittings and sculpture.

The skills that I learned whilst building furniture have enabled me to gradually develop more complex instruments over the past decade. Usually an instrument takes between 2-3 years to develop and several months of construction in the workshop. I will sketch hundreds of ideas based on my observations of other acoustic instruments. They become metallic hybrids of more traditional instruments. I combine three or four essential ideas into an interface of strings, springs, idiophones and bowing mechanisms.

I am always looking for unusual environments in which to perform and record in.

My search has taken me further into Melbourne’s sub-terrain, into locations such as storm water pipes, bridges and other industrial installations.

I love exploring the relationship between the acoustic qualities inherent in the chosen locations and the timbre of my instruments. Electronic sound and sounds of these remote locations are incorporated via the microphone, together with prepared tapes and computer compositions. Compositions based solely on recordings of these spaces are arranged using portable mini-disc Omni-direction microphones and contact transducers.

Since 2000 my performance practice has focussed more on the live stage, usually at venues that promote an emphasis for improvisation.

Last year I was awarded a grant through the instrument builder’s scheme of the Australia Council for the Arts and have spent most of this year developing prototypes and an instrument, which features continuos bowing mechanism.

I perform regularly through out Australia and form part of the Make it up Club committee where I also act as a curator.
