Scar - A Stolen Vision (2001)
Related EntriesAudio Visual Gallery
Outdoor Installation
Location: Melbourne City Square, Victoria, Australia
Installation by Victorian-based Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander artists including Kimba Thompson, Karen Casey, Ray Thomas, Maree Clarke, Glenn Romanis, Craig Charles, Ricardo Idagi and Treahna Hamm. Sound design by Peter Mumme
Details
URL: The home page for this entity is located at http://www.abc.net.au/message/blackarts/visual/s643619.htm
Additional Information
'Scar Tree', a public exhibition, had to do with an indigenous view of contemporary Australian society - as expressed through the sculpting of 32(reclaimed ex-pier) poles. An attendant soundtrack was required to compliment and extend the visuals, incorporating stories of the sculptor’s experiences and influences.

The intention was to 'bring the poles to life', having them 'tell' of their own making and, in turn, their sculptor's stories. We constructed the soundtrack around a 'flowing' theme - starting on the Murray River and ending up at sea! There were numerous references made to this river - and to the sea; both having large influences on several of the artists involved in the project. See score example.

There was a complaint about (possible) 'noise' level delivered to MCC - before we had even started the soundtrack for this project! Located on the corner of Collins and Swanston Streets in Melbourne, the background levels were pretty high and full spectrum. The soundtrack was kept within information/vocal frequency range, filtering off everything else. We used short-throw horn loaded loudspeakers for the playback, in pairs, with each pair driven by (frequency) band limited tracks sourced off CDs.

Our initial idea was to mount loudspeakers onto the sculptured poles - but this didn't get too far, so we settled for mounting the speakers adjacent too, and over, the installation - see drawings. We were able to 'beam' the replay sound sufficiently so as to create distinct listening areas for each of eight tracks; this creating a 'walk through' effect. We recorded the sculpting of the poles, contact mics were used as they were being carved, sawed, burned, chiselled and ground; these sounds were later manipulated and placed back within the recreated acoustic space of the construction wood yard.

Electronic system design by Dex Audio.
