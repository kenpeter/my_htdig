Transparency (2001)
Related Entries
Gallery Installation and Immersive Environment
Location: Fondation Cartier pour l’art contemporain, Paris, France
By Philip Samartzis
Details
Transparency – 16-channel sound installation presented at Fondation Cartier pour l’art contemporain, Paris. (July 3rd - July 8th 2001)

The intention of Transparency is to analyse a range of sounds derived from a survey of private and public spaces and compose a work comprised of the transparent sounds that occupy these familiar, everyday places, presented throughout the garden of an invisible building. Sounds that are either ignored, unheard or forgotten. Sounds on the verge of silence that occasionally swell from the noise threshold of perceptibility to permeate spaces like perfume. Refrigerator noises, fluorescent lights, air conditioning, vibrating pipes, distant conversations, footsteps and traffic rumble to name some. An on-site investigation led to the formation of a comprehensive database divided into frequency, intensity, rhythm, mass and texture. This was achieved by either directly recording specific events, or through simulation mediated by software. The objective was to develop a spectro-morphology of sound predicated on certain re-occurring characteristics common to all domestic and work environments.
